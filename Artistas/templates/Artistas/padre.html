Grunge

Grunge, sometimes known as the Seattle sound, is a subgenre of alternative rock influenced by punk, hardcore punk, noise rock, heavy metal and with structures close to classic pop rock. It emerged in the late 1980s, with groups coming primarily from the US state of Washington, in particular the Seattle area. The first company that promoted and made the genre known was the Sub Pop record company, supporting bands that would be fundamental in the development of the nascent genre, such as Nirvana, Green River, Pearl Jam, Soundgarden and Alice in Chains. The distinctive characteristics of the grunge sound were its strongly distorted and energetic guitars, vocal melodies, often catchy and repetitive, as well as predominant drums; while his lyrics were characterized by reflecting apathy and disenchantment

Punk

Punk emerged in the mid-1970s. This genre is characterized in the music industry by its independent and countercultural attitude. In its beginnings, punk was a music of minimalist composition, very simple and crude, and sometimes careless: a type of simple rock, with aggressive melodies of short durations, sounds of amplified guitars that were not very controlled and noisy, loaded with a lot of distortion, few arrangements and instruments and usually fast beats and tempos. Punk is the constant fight against the fear of social repercussions.

Funk

Funk was born in the mid-to-late 1960s, when primarily African-American musicians fused soul, jazz, Latin rhythms (mambo, for example), and R&B into a new rhythmic, danceable musical form. Funk reduces the prominence of the melody and harmony and gives, in return, more weight to the percussion and the bass line. Like much of African-American music, funk is often built on a complex groove created from rhythm instruments. Funk was a decisive influence in the development of disco music and Afrobeat. On the other hand, funk samples have been widely used in genres such as hip hop, house, and drum and bass. When Funk is mixed with a bit of Punk and other Rock additions, it is where it really gains another type of interest for the new generations.
